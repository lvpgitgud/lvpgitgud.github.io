Overview

I've always been fascinated with the field of Computer Graphics, not long ago, I was first discovered OpenGL while developing one of my first game , since then I have dreamt of maintaining my own ad hoc renderer or game engine of some kind. The led me down a rabbit hole of learning more about graphics programming, the graphics pipeline and GPU/SIMD architecture. This project is an accumulation of my knowledge put to use.

About Vulkan

OpenGL's was the leading graphics library, back then, the people behind OpenGL announced NextGl, the supposed successor of OpenGL, and now it is here and it's called Vulkan.
Vulkan can do anything OpenGL can and so much more.. at the cost of a high learning curve.

One of the main appeal of Vulkan to me is its similiar syntax to OpenGL and Raytracing hardware acceleration support, which is a topic I wished to pursue in the foreseeable future.

Can't make an omlette without breaking eggs

In order for me to fully explore the potential of Vulkan, first I need to build a graphics pipeline using Vulkan, which is a task easier said than done.

Luckily for me, the Kronos Group, the ones behind Vulkan, was graciously enough to provide a hands-on tutorial for their new API, which I highly recommend.
